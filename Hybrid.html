Hybrid Energy Optimisation – Power-Split HEV

Improving energy efficiency of a hybrid electric vehicle on the WLTP cycle using advanced control strategies.

Context

Hybrid vehicles, such as the Toyota Prius, use a power-split device to balance power between an internal combustion engine and two electric motors. This project focused on optimising energy usage in a simulated hybrid vehicle while maintaining driveability under the WLTP cycle.

Objective

To reduce overall energy consumption and emissions in a power-split HEV model by modifying engine start-stop control and adjusting engine operating regions.

Methodology

  • Designed a composite start-stop control logic based on battery state-of-charge (SOC) and vehicle speed.
  • Increased minimum torque and engine speed thresholds to avoid low-efficiency operating zones.
  • Simulated drive cycles in Simulink using a backward-forward energy flow model.
  • Attempted optimisation using Simulink's Response Optimiser (manual tuning yielded better results).

Battery State of Charge over WLTP Cycle

Results

  • Engine starts reduced: 61 → 9 (↓ 85%)
  • Fuel energy usage: 22.2 MJ → 21.74 MJ (↓ 2.1%)
  • Battery energy usage: 0.326 kWh → 0.167 kWh (↓ 48.6%)

Insights

This project deepened my understanding of hybrid control strategies, SOC-dependent logic, and trade-offs between energy sources. It also highlighted the importance of setting cost functions when using automatic tuning tools.
