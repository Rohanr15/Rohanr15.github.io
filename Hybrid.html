Hybrid Vehicle Modelling and Analysis

Model-based energy optimisation for a power-split hybrid architecture

Objective

To optimise total energy consumption (fuel and battery) for a vehicle using a power-split hybrid drivetrain, while reducing emissions and preserving drivability.

Tools & Approach

  • Used the ELVIO Simulink toolbox to simulate the hybrid powertrain.
  • Implemented a start-stop control strategy triggered by battery SOC and vehicle speed.
  • Adjusted operational speed and torque setpoints using the Simulink Response Optimiser.

Results

  • Battery energy consumption reduced by 48%
  • Fuel energy usage dropped by 2.1%
  • Engine starts reduced from 61 to 9

Simulation Diagram

Key Takeaways

This project strengthened my understanding of hybrid energy flow, supervisory control strategies, and multi-objective optimisation using MATLAB/Simulink.
