Energy Absorbers for Crashworthiness

Finite element simulation of a frontal impact to evaluate the crash performance of a vehicle’s structural energy absorber.

Objective

To predict the deceleration-time (crash pulse) response of a vehicle energy absorber (front longitudinal steel tube) during a frontal impact at 60 km/h into a rigid wall. The aim was to ensure passenger safety by maintaining low deceleration levels.

Simulation Setup

  • Software used: MSC Marc – a nonlinear FEA solver for crash and contact problems.
  • Model: Rectangular steel tube representing front longitudinal member of a SMART Fortwo.
  • Boundary Conditions: Tube pushed against a fixed wall using equivalent vehicle mass.
  • Material: Mild steel with defined yield strength, plasticity, and strain-rate effects.

Procedure

The simulation was set up as a dynamic transient analysis. Contact algorithms were defined between the tube and the rigid wall. The model was meshed using solid elements, and simulation time steps were chosen to ensure numerical stability.

Results

  • Vehicle speed successfully reduced from 60 km/h to zero over a short time duration.
  • Crash pulse (deceleration vs. time) stayed within injury-prevention thresholds.
  • Crushing force vs. displacement graph showed energy absorption trend matching physical test results.
  • Fully crushed shape matched expectations from published research.
