Spark-Ignition Engine Testing

Investigating the impact of spark timing on engine performance using MATLAB-based analysis of test data from a Ford Sigma SI engine.

Objective

To study how varying the spark timing affects cylinder pressure and overall engine performance characteristics in a spark-ignition internal combustion engine.

Test Setup

  • Engine tested: Ford Sigma spark-ignition (SI) engine
  • Constant engine speed maintained during all tests
  • Data collected across a range of spark advance angles and dynamometer loads

Procedure

Engine output was monitored for each spark timing configuration. MATLAB was used for data import, processing, and visualisation. Calculations were performed to derive performance parameters such as indicated mean effective pressure (IMEP), thermal efficiency, and peak cylinder pressure.

Results

  • Identified optimal spark timing that maximised cylinder pressure and thermal efficiency.
  • Understood how spark advance influences engine knocking and power output.
  • Gained hands-on experience with testbed equipment and engine dynamometer control systems.
