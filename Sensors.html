Assessment of Vehicle Sensors

Comparing onboard vehicle sensors with high-precision GPS/IMU equipment for dynamic performance analysis.

Objective

To evaluate the accuracy of CANBUS sensor data from a test vehicle against specialised measurement systems such as the OXTS RT3000, particularly for dynamic quantities like yaw rate and lateral acceleration.

Test Setup

  • Test conducted on MIRA proving ground using a fully instrumented vehicle.
  • Measurements included yaw rate, lateral and longitudinal acceleration, GPS position, and velocity.
  • Sensors: Vehicleâ€™s native CANBUS vs. OXTS RT3000 (high-grade GPS/IMU).

Procedure

Data from both sources was synchronised and compared. MATLAB was used for data processing and integration:

  • Yaw rate and lateral acceleration were integrated to derive yaw angle and lateral velocity.
  • GPS data was processed using the Haversine formula to recreate the vehicle path on the test track.

Results

  • Vehicle sensors accurately tracked yaw rate and lateral acceleration within minor tolerances.
  • GPS-based reconstruction of the vehicle path aligned closely with expected trajectories.
  • Revealed the importance of sensor fusion for autonomous driving applications.
