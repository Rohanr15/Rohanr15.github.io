Battery Pack Specification for a BEV

Designing an optimised battery pack to meet performance and range targets for electric vehicles.

Objective

To develop a battery pack specification capable of:

  • Providing a 300-mile range at 65 mph cruising speed
  • Delivering 150 kW peak power output for at least 20 seconds
  • Balancing energy, performance, and total pack weight

Procedure

  • Compared two different Li-ion cell types using experimental data
  • Calculated required pack size based on energy and power demands
  • Estimated weight, capacity, and configuration for each candidate
  • Selected the most efficient solution while meeting all constraints

Voltage Curves Comparison

Results

  • Cell A was selected, resulting in a pack 53.8 kg lighter than with Cell B
  • All required energy and performance targets were met
  • Offered deeper insight into battery design, energy density, and pack integration trade-offs

Key Takeaways

This project deepened my understanding of EV battery system design, including the relationship between cell chemistry, energy requirements, and vehicle performance. It highlighted the role of engineering trade-offs and system-level thinking in electric powertrain design.
