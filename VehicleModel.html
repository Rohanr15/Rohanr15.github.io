Vehicle Modelling – Coastdown Test

Integrating experimental coastdown test data with a Simulink model for performance prediction and parameter estimation.

Objective

To obtain real-world experience of straight-line vehicle performance and use a simulation model to replicate a coastdown test. The aim was to estimate vehicle drag and rolling resistance parameters by minimising the error between measured and simulated wheel speeds.

Test Procedure

The coastdown test was conducted on the HORIBA MIRA test track using a 2015 Ford Focus ST. The car was accelerated to a target speed, the clutch was disengaged, and it was allowed to coast to a stop. Data was recorded at two speeds (70 mph and 50 mph) and in both directions to average out wind effects.

  • Data included accelerator/clutch pedal positions, air temperature, and gear indication.
  • Multiple runs were conducted for statistical accuracy.

Modelling Procedure

A vehicle simulation model was developed in Simulink. The Simulink Parameter Estimation tool was used to tune key coefficients:

  • Aerodynamic drag coefficient (Cd)
  • Rolling resistance (velocity-dependent and constant terms)

The tool defined an objective function to minimise the root-mean-squared error (RMSE) between simulated and measured wheel speeds.

Results

  • Model RMSE reduced by 59%, indicating strong agreement with real-world data.
  • Gained experience in integrating physical testing with simulation.
  • Collaborated in a team to capture and process test data using ATI Vision software.
